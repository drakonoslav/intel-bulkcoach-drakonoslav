Below is a full BulkCoach v1 system spec for the three-layer oscillator model.

This is written as a product/logic spec, not just advice. It is meant to be implementable in your app and stable enough to refine over months and years.

⸻

BulkCoach Androgen Oscillator System Spec

Version

v1.0

Purpose

Use daily Apple Health data, body-composition data, waist data, subjective libido/readiness data, and training data to classify each day into a recovery-performance state and automatically govern:
	•	cardio mode (Zone 2 / Zone 3 / recovery walk)
	•	lifting mode (neural / hypertrophy / pump / recovery)
	•	macro day type (surge / build / reset / resensitize)
	•	28-day rhythm progression
	•	long-horizon adaptation review

The system has three nested layers:
	1.	Acute Score — fast layer, 0–3 days
	2.	Resource Score — medium layer, 1–6 weeks
	3.	Seasonal Score — slow layer, 1–12 months

Composite output:
	•	Oscillator Composite Score (OCS) 0–100

The system does not diagnose hormones.
It models training-readiness, resource sufficiency, and re-peak capacity.

⸻

1. Core design philosophy

Fixed anchors

These should stay stable unless there is a deliberate schedule change:
	•	wake time
	•	sleep window
	•	cardio start time
	•	lift start time
	•	meal timing anchors
	•	protein floor
	•	weekly review cadence
	•	28-day cycle cadence

Oscillating variables

These deliberately vary to preserve sensitivity:
	•	carb:fat ratio
	•	Zone 2 vs Zone 3 frequency
	•	lift density
	•	lift intensity
	•	deload depth
	•	stress amplitude
	•	surplus amplitude
	•	recovery emphasis

3. Normalization framework

Every variable should be converted to a 0–100 subscore or similar normalized form.

Use three kinds of baseline:

3.1 Fixed baseline

For values with a meaningful personal long-run baseline:
	•	yearly HRV average
	•	yearly RHR average
	•	target waist
	•	target calories

3.2 Rolling baseline

For fast-changing signals:
	•	7-day HRV average
	•	7-day RHR average
	•	7-day sleep average
	•	7-day weight average

3.3 Reference trend baseline

For medium/slow signals:
	•	14-day waist average
	•	14-day FFM trend
	•	28-day HRV trend
	•	28-day sleep regularity trend

4. Acute Score specification

4.1 Purpose

Detect today’s near-term capacity to absorb stress and perform.

Time horizon:
	•	current day
	•	previous 1–3 days

Range:
	•	0 to 100

4.2 Acute Score components and weights

Component	Weight
HRV state	22
RHR state	18
Sleep quantity	15
Sleep regularity	8
Bodyweight stability	5
Subjective drive/libido	10
Joint/soreness state	10
Yesterday training strain	7
Yesterday cardio strain	5

Total = 100

⸻

4.3 Component formulas

A. HRV state (0–22)

Use both yearly baseline and 7-day reference.

Definitions:
	•	hrv_today
	•	hrv_7d_avg
	•	hrv_year_avg

Primary ratio:
	•	hrv_ratio = hrv_today / max(hrv_7d_avg, 1)

Secondary ratio:
	•	hrv_year_ratio = hrv_today / max(hrv_year_avg, 1)

Scoring:
	•	if hrv_ratio >= 1.10, score = 22
	•	if 1.03–1.09, score = 19
	•	if 0.97–1.02, score = 15
	•	if 0.90–0.96, score = 9
	•	if <0.90, score = 4

Adjustment:
	•	if hrv_year_ratio > 1.15, add +1 capped at 22
	•	if hrv_year_ratio < 0.85, subtract -1 floored at 0

⸻

B. RHR state (0–18)

Definitions:
	•	rhr_today
	•	rhr_7d_avg
	•	rhr_year_avg

Delta:
	•	rhr_delta = rhr_today - rhr_7d_avg

Scoring:
	•	<= -3 bpm → 18
	•	-2 to -1 → 16
	•	0 to +1 → 13
	•	+2 to +3 → 8
	•	+4 to +5 → 4
	•	> +5 → 1

Adjustment:
	•	if rhr_today <= rhr_year_avg - 3, +1 capped 18
	•	if rhr_today >= rhr_year_avg + 5, -1 floored 0

⸻

C. Sleep quantity (0–15)

Target:
	•	optimal band = 450–510 min (7.5–8.5 hr)
	•	acceptable = 420–540 min

Scoring:
	•	450–510 → 15
	•	420–449 or 511–540 → 12
	•	390–419 → 8
	•	360–389 → 4
	•	<360 → 1

Optional bonus:
	•	if efficiency >= 90%, add +1 capped 15

⸻

D. Sleep regularity (0–8)

Use midpoint shift from 7-day midpoint average.
	•	midpoint_shift_min = abs(sleep_midpoint_today - sleep_midpoint_7d_avg)

Scoring:
	•	<= 20 min → 8
	•	21–40 → 6
	•	41–60 → 4
	•	61–90 → 2
	•	>90 → 0

⸻

E. Bodyweight stability (0–5)

Purpose:
detect abrupt dehydration/inflammation swings.
	•	weight_delta_pct = (weight_today - weight_7d_avg) / weight_7d_avg

Scoring:
	•	abs(delta) <= 0.4% → 5
	•	0.41–0.8% → 4
	•	0.81–1.2% → 2
	•	>1.2% → 0

⸻

F. Subjective drive/libido (0–10)

Use average of:
	•	libido_score
	•	morning_erection_score normalized
	•	motivation_score
	•	mental_drive_score

Normalization:
convert each to 0–10 scale, then average.

Example:
	•	libido 1–5 becomes (value-1)/4*10
	•	erection 0–3 becomes value/3*10

Final subjective subscore capped 10.

⸻

G. Joint/soreness state (0–10)

Use reverse-coded:
	•	soreness_score
	•	joint_friction_score

Example:
	•	very low soreness and low friction = 10
	•	moderate = 6
	•	high = 2

⸻

H. Yesterday lift strain (0–7)

Use prior-day lift_strain_score 0–100.

Scoring:
	•	strain 0–35 → 7
	•	36–55 → 5
	•	56–70 → 3
	•	70 → 1

This is not “hard lift bad,” just acute recovery cost.

⸻

I. Yesterday cardio strain (0–5)

Use prior-day cardio mode:
	•	recovery walk → 5
	•	zone 2 → 4
	•	zone 3 short → 3
	•	zone 3 full → 2
	•	extra interval or prolonged high intensity → 1

⸻

	•	max 100

5. Resource Score specification

5.1 Purpose

Detect whether the body is sufficiently fueled, recovering, and structurally trending well to continue growth.

Time horizon:
	•	7 to 42 days

Range:
	•	0 to 100

5.2 Resource Score components and weights

Component	Weight
Calorie adherence 7d	10
Protein adequacy 7d	12
Fat floor adherence 7d	12
Carb adequacy around training	10
Weight trend	10
Waist trend	12
FFM trend	12
Strength trend	12
Cardio monotony penalty adjusted score	10

Total = 100

⸻

5.3 Component formulas

A. Calorie adherence 7d (0–10)

Use 7-day average intake vs 7-day target.
	•	kcal_ratio = kcal_7d_avg / kcal_target_7d_avg

Scoring:
	•	0.97–1.03 → 10
	•	0.94–0.96 or 1.04–1.06 → 8
	•	0.90–0.93 or 1.07–1.10 → 5
	•	otherwise → 2

⸻

B. Protein adequacy 7d (0–12)

Target range:
	•	170–180 g/day average

Scoring:
	•	within target range → 12
	•	160–169 or 181–190 → 9
	•	145–159 → 5
	•	below 145 → 1

Could also use % adherence if dynamic targets later.

⸻

C. Fat floor adherence 7d (0–12)

This is important for your design.

Minimum preferred weekly average:
	•	>= 55 g/day
Better:
	•	some days 75–95g and some days 35–45g

Two checks:

1. Average floor
	•	average fat >= 55g → pass

2. Oscillation presence
	•	at least 2 days >= 75g and at least 2 days <= 50g → oscillation bonus

Scoring:
	•	floor pass + oscillation pass → 12
	•	floor pass only → 9
	•	average 50–54g → 6
	•	average <50g → 2

⸻

D. Carb adequacy around training (0–10)

Use average carbs placed in:
	•	post-cardio meal
	•	pre-lift meal
	•	post-lift meal

Target training-window carbs:
	•	build day: 180–240g across those windows
	•	reset day: 120–170g
	•	surge day: 220–280g

Score by day-type adherence averaged across 7d:
	•	=90% adherence → 10
	•	80–89% → 8
	•	65–79% → 5
	•	<65% → 2

⸻

E. Weight trend (0–10)

Use 14-day weight trend.

For recomposition / lean-gain phase:
	•	preferred rate: +0.10 to +0.50 lb/week
	•	maintenance phase: -0.10 to +0.20 lb/week
This should be phase-aware.

In v1, assume lean-gain default:
	•	+0.10 to +0.50 lb/wk → 10
	•	stable to +0.09 → 8
	•	+0.51 to +0.80 → 6
	•	negative trend below -0.10 → 4
	•	+0.80 → 3

⸻

F. Waist trend (0–12)

Use 14-day average waist trend.

Target:
	•	stable or slight decrease
	•	small increase tolerated during expansion

Scoring:
	•	-0.25 in to +0.10 in over 14d → 12
	•	+0.11 to +0.25 → 9
	•	+0.26 to +0.40 → 6
	•	+0.40 → 2

If no fresh waist within 10 days:
	•	use last known but cap score at 8

⸻

G. FFM trend (0–12)

Use 14-day or 28-day smoothed FFM.

Scoring:
	•	+0.20 to +0.60 lb/week → 12
	•	+0.05 to +0.19 → 9
	•	stable → 7
	•	negative but <= -0.20 → 3

If body comp confidence is low, blend with weight+waist logic.

⸻

H. Strength trend (0–12)

Use main-lift output trend or strength index trend.

Suggested formula:
	•	compare 14-day average strength_output_index vs prior 14-day average

Scoring:
	•	improvement >2% → 12
	•	stable 0 to +2% → 9
	•	slight dip -0.1 to -2% → 6
	•	worse than -2% → 2

⸻

I. Cardio monotony penalty adjusted score (0–10)

Purpose:
punish doing the same intensity every day.

Compute 7-day cardio distribution:
	•	number of Zone 2 days
	•	number of Zone 3 days
	•	number of recovery/easy days

Ideal weekly distribution:
	•	Z2 = 3
	•	Z3 = 3
	•	easy = 1

Scoring:
	•	exact 3/3/1 or close 3/2/2 or 2/3/2 → 10
	•	moderate imbalance but not flat → 7
	•	5+ days same zone → 4
	•	7 identical days → 1

⸻

6.1 Purpose

Detect whether the system is becoming more resilient and more re-peak capable across months.

Time horizon:
	•	28 days to 12 months

Range:
	•	0 to 100

6.2 Seasonal Score components and weights

Component	Weight
HRV 28d trend	18
RHR 28d trend	14
Sleep regularity 28d trend	10
Waist:weight relationship	12
FFM 28d trend	14
Deload compliance	10
Training monotony index	8
Light / outdoor consistency	6
Motivation / virility trend	8

Total = 100

⸻

6.3 Component formulas

A. HRV 28d trend (0–18)

Compare latest 28-day mean to previous 28-day mean.
	•	improvement > +8% → 18
	•	+3 to +8% → 15
	•	stable ±2% → 11
	•	-3 to -7% → 6
	•	worse than -7% → 2

⸻

B. RHR 28d trend (0–14)

Compare latest 28-day mean to previous 28-day mean.
	•	improvement <= -3 bpm → 14
	•	-1 to -2 bpm → 11
	•	stable → 8
	•	+1 to +2 bpm → 4
	•	+2 bpm → 1

⸻

C. Sleep regularity 28d trend (0–10)

Use average midpoint deviation / bedtime consistency.
	•	markedly improved → 10
	•	somewhat improved → 8
	•	stable good → 7
	•	erratic → 3
	•	worsening → 1

⸻

D. Waist:weight relationship (0–12)

Purpose:
did weight gain mostly preserve shape?

Compute:
	•	waist_change_per_lb = waist_28d_change / max(weight_28d_change, 0.5)

Interpretation:
lower is better.

Scoring:
	•	gaining weight with waist stable or down → 12
	•	slight waist rise with bodyweight rise → 9
	•	waist rising faster than expected → 5
	•	waist rising without productive weight trend → 2

⸻

E. FFM 28d trend (0–14)

Compare latest 28-day FFM average to previous 28-day average.
	•	clear positive trend → 14
	•	mild positive → 11
	•	stable → 8
	•	negative → 3

⸻

F. Deload compliance (0–10)

At least one meaningful resensitization block per 28 days:
	•	volume down 35–50%
	•	more Z2
	•	fewer surge days
	•	higher recovery emphasis

Scoring:
	•	full deload done → 10
	•	partial → 6
	•	none → 1

⸻

G. Training monotony index (0–8)

Use weekly variability in:
	•	lift types
	•	cardio modes
	•	macro day types

Scoring:
	•	healthy variation → 8
	•	moderate variation → 5
	•	flat identical weeks → 1

⸻

H. Light/outdoor consistency (0–6)

Use Apple outdoor time or manual morning light exposure log.
	•	consistent daily light exposure → 6
	•	moderate → 4
	•	low → 2

⸻

I. Motivation/virility trend (0–8)

Use 28-day trend in:
	•	libido_score
	•	motivation_score
	•	mood_stability_score
	•	morning_erection_score if logged
	•	positive trend → 8
	•	stable good → 6
	•	stable mediocre → 4
	•	worsening → 1

⸻

7. Oscillator Composite Score

7.1 Formula

ocs =
  (acute_score * 0.50)
+ (resource_score * 0.30)
+ (seasonal_score * 0.20)

Round to nearest integer 0–100.

7.2 Classification bands

OCS	Class	Meaning
85–100	Peak	highest-value day
70–84	Strong Build	push is supported
55–69	Controlled Build	productive but not maximal
40–54	Reset	reduce neural cost
0–39	Resensitize	recovery priority

8. Daily decision rules

These convert score into action.

8.1 Cardio decision

If OCS >= 85
	•	default cardio = Zone 3
	•	unless prior 2 days already Zone 3, then switch to Zone 2

If OCS 70–84
	•	Zone 3 allowed if:
	•	HRV subscore good
	•	RHR not elevated
	•	previous day not Zone 3 + heavy neural lift
	•	otherwise Zone 2

If OCS 55–69
	•	default cardio = Zone 2

If OCS 40–54
	•	Zone 2 short or brisk walk

If OCS < 40
	•	easy walk / recovery only

⸻

8.2 Lift-type decision

Peak (85–100)
	•	lift_type = neural_tension
	•	heavy compounds
	•	lower rep
	•	longer rest
	•	fewer total exercises

Strong Build (70–84)
	•	lift_type = hypertrophy_build
	•	moderate reps
	•	full productive session

Controlled Build (55–69)
	•	lift_type = pump_or_moderate_hypertrophy
	•	less axial fatigue
	•	avoid grind

Reset (40–54)
	•	lift_type = recovery_patterning
	•	lighter
	•	technique / mobility / light pump

Resensitize (<40)
	•	lift_type = no_hard_lift
	•	walk, mobility, optional easy blood flow

⸻

8.3 Macro day-type decision

Peak
	•	macro_day = surge

Strong Build
	•	macro_day = build

Controlled Build
	•	macro_day = build or reset
	•	choose reset if RHR elevated or soreness high

Reset
	•	macro_day = reset

Resensitize
	•	macro_day = resensitize

9. Zone 2 / Zone 3 auto-switch logic

This should not be based on OCS alone. It must also enforce weekly rhythm.

9.1 Weekly target distribution

Per 7 days:
	•	Zone 3 target = 3 days
	•	Zone 2 target = 3 days
	•	Recovery/easy target = 1 day

9.2 Rule stack

Rule 1: hard stop fatigue rules

Force Zone 2 or recovery if any:
	•	HRV < 90% of 7d average
	•	RHR > 7d average + 4 bpm
	•	sleep < 6 hr
	•	joint+soreness subscore low
	•	OCS < 55

Rule 2: protect weekly balance

If already completed:
	•	3 Zone 3 days in current rolling 7 days
then next cardio defaults to Zone 2 unless OCS >= 90 and no fatigue flags.

Rule 3: prevent monotony

If last 4 cardio sessions were same zone:
	•	switch next eligible session to other zone

Rule 4: peak utilization

If OCS >= 85 and Zone 3 count < 3 in rolling 7d:
	•	assign Zone 3

Rule 5: resensitize week override

During days 22–28 of monthly cycle, cap Zone 3 to max 1 day unless OCS extremely high and recovery markers excellent.

10. Macro day templates

These are day-type templates.

10.1 Surge day

Use on Peak days.
	•	Protein: 175 g
	•	Carbs: 390 g
	•	Fat: 40 g
	•	Calories: ~2680–2720

Meal emphasis:
	•	very high training-window carbs
	•	low fat pre/post training

10.2 Build day

Use on Strong Build days.
	•	Protein: 175 g
	•	Carbs: 340 g
	•	Fat: 55 g
	•	Calories: ~2585–2660 depending food labels and rounding

To keep near 2695, you can also use:
	•	175 / 350 / 60

10.3 Reset day

Use on Reset days.
	•	Protein: 175 g
	•	Carbs: 280 g
	•	Fat: 80 g
	•	Calories: ~2580–2660 depending exact numbers

Closer to 2695 option:
	•	175 / 290 / 80

10.4 Resensitize day

Use on low-score or deep recovery days.
	•	Protein: 175 g
	•	Carbs: 230 g
	•	Fat: 95 g
	•	Calories: ~2515–2615

Alternative closer-to-baseline version:
	•	175 / 250 / 90

15. Derived helper formulas

15.1 Sleep regularity score helper

sleep_regularity_score =
max(0, 100 - (abs(sleep_midpoint_today - sleep_midpoint_7d_avg) / 90) * 100)

Clamp 0–100.

15.2 Weight trend lb/week

Use linear regression over 14 days.

weight_trend_lb_per_week = slope(weight over 14d) * 7

15.3 FFM trend lb/week

Same method:

ffm_trend_lb_per_week = slope(ffm over 14d) * 7

15.4 Strength output index

Simple v1 option:

strength_output_index =
(top_set_load_index * 0.5)
+ (session_density_score * 0.2)
+ (rep_speed_subjective_score * 0.1)
+ (pump_quality_score * 0.2)

Could later be exercise-weighted.

17. Adjustment rules

Raise calories by 75–100 kcal if:
	•	Resource Score stays >75 for 3+ weeks
	•	FFM trend flat
	•	waist stable
	•	strength trend positive but stalled recently

Lower calories by 75–100 kcal if:
	•	waist climbs >0.4 in over 14d
	•	FFM not improving
	•	weight rises too fast
	•	Resource Score declines despite adherence

Increase Zone 2 proportion if:
	•	HRV compressed for >10 days
	•	RHR elevated
	•	OCS rarely exceeds 75
	•	Zone 3 feels stale

Increase surge day frequency slightly if:
	•	Seasonal score good
	•	recovery excellent
	•	strength and FFM trending up
	•	waist stable

	•	contrast between high-carb and high-fat days
